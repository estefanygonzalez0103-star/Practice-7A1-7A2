Full Analysis — Temperature Data
🌡️ A scientist records daily high temperatures (°C) for 2 weeks:
24, 27, 22, 29, 31, 25, 28, 26, 30, 23, 27, 25, 29, 27
Step 1 — What is the MODE temperature?
Count each: 27 appears 3 times (days 2, 11, 14). No other temperature appears that often.
Step 2 — The sum of all 14 temperatures is 372°C. What is the MEAN?
372 ÷ 14 = 26.57... ≈ 26.6°C
Step 3 — Sorted: 22,23,24,25,25,26,27,27,27,28,29,29,30,31. Find the MEDIAN (14 values → average 7th and 8th).
7th value=27, 8th value=27. (27+27)÷2 = 27.
Step 4 — What is the RANGE of temperatures?
Max=31, Min=22. Range = 31−22 = ?

Challenge — Mean and Range Together
🎯 Five students took a test. The mean score is 78. Four of the scores are 85, 72, 90, and 68. The range of all five scores is 30.
Step 1 — What is the fifth score? (Use the mean to find it)
Show your work
Total = 78×5 = 390. Known = 85+72+90+68 = 315. Fifth = 390−315 = ?
Step 2 — All five scores are: 85, 72, 90, 68, 75. What is the highest score?
Step 3 — The range is 30. If the highest score is 90, what is the lowest score? (Range = highest − lowest, so lowest = highest − range)
If Range = Max − Min, then Min = Max − Range = 90 − 30 = ?
Wait — does 60 appear in the data? No! This means the range of 30 refers to a different extreme. The actual lowest is 68, and the range would be 90−68=22. So the stated range of 30 means min=60, which is NOT in our list — this tests if you understand the formula!
